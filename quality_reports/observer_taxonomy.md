# Board Observer Taxonomy

## Who Are Board Observers? A Complete Picture

Based on 4,915 unique individuals identified as board observers across 3,058 companies
in S&P Capital IQ Professionals (title LIKE '%observer%' AND boardflag = 1).

Filtered to 2,942 US private company observers at 1,830 companies.

---

## Observer Employers: Where Do They Work?

Of the 2,942 US private company observers:

| Employer Type | Observers | % | Description |
|--------------|-----------|---|-------------|
| **Traditional VC/PE** | 1,857 | 63.1% | Private Investment Firm, Public Investment Firm, Private Fund |
| **Corporate Venture Capital** | 153 | 5.2% | Corporate Investment Arms (Intel Capital, Google Ventures, Qualcomm, etc.) |
| **Financial Service Investment** | 16 | 0.5% | Financial service arms (Citi Ventures, GE Equity, Susquehanna) |
| **Other Investment-Named** | ~50 | 1.7% | Investment firms classified as "Private Company" in CIQ |
| **Academia** | ~200 | 6.8% | Business school professors (Kellogg, MIT Sloan, UCLA Anderson, Stanford GSB) |
| **Other Startups** | ~400 | 13.6% | Founders/executives at other private companies |
| **Public Companies** | ~170 | 5.8% | Directors/officers at public companies |
| **Other** | ~96 | 3.3% | Foundations, government, trade associations |

### Key Insight
Only 63% of observers are traditional VC/PE — the rest come from corporate venture arms, academia, other startups, and public companies. The observer role is NOT exclusively a VC governance tool.

## Traditional VC/PE Observers (63.1%)

The largest category. These are investors who negotiate observer seats as part of financing transactions.

**Top VC firms by observer connections:**
- New Enterprise Associates (38 observed companies)
- Kleiner Perkins (28)
- Domain Associates (27)
- ARCH Venture Partners (25)
- The Carlyle Group (24)
- Atlas Venture (23)
- Battery Ventures (23)

**Characteristics:**
- Typically placed at Series A or later
- Observer seat negotiated in Investor Rights Agreement
- Observer is usually a partner or principal at the VC firm
- Full information access, no fiduciary duty (per Obasi v. Tibet Pharma)
- 82% of VC entities use observers (NVCA Q4 2023 survey)

Business school professors and administrators who serve on startup boards.

**Top institutions:**
| Institution | Observers |
|------------|-----------|
| Kellogg School of Management (Northwestern) | 9 |
| UCLA Anderson School of Management | 5 |
| MIT Sloan School of Management | 4 |
| Stanford Graduate School of Business | ~3 |
| Harvard Business School | ~3 |
| Indian Institute of Management (Ahmedabad/Bangalore) | 3 |
| Carroll School of Management (Boston College) | 2 |

**Characteristics:**
- Often serve as advisors/mentors rather than investor representatives
- May be affiliated with university venture funds or accelerators
- Bring domain expertise rather than capital
- Likely have different information dynamics than VC observers

---

## Cross-Startup Observers (~13.6%)

Founders/executives at other private companies who observe at a different startup.

**Characteristics:**
- Serial entrepreneurs observing at their former or allied companies
- May reflect personal investment (angel) rather than institutional
- Co-founders who step back to observer role
- Titles include: "Co-Founder & Board Observer", "Founder & Observer"

---

## Public Company Directors (~5.8%)

Directors or officers at public companies who also observe at private companies.

**Characteristics:**
- The "information bridge" from private to public — the original hypothesis of this paper
- Only 339 positions (11.7% of non-observed positions)
- 97% are "Former" — most have left the public company

## Key Takeaway for the Paper

The observer institution is broader than the VC governance literature recognizes. While 63% of observers are VC/PE-placed, a significant minority comes from corporate venture capital (5%), academia (7%), other startups (14%), and public companies (6%). This diversity suggests that board observer seats serve multiple functions beyond VC monitoring — including strategic intelligence for corporations, mentorship from academics, and cross-pollination between startups.

The paper's analysis focuses on the VC/PE + CVC subset (68% of observers) where the investment relationship is clearest and the information channel is most economically significant.
