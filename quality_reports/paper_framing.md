## Paper Framing: Information Permeability of the Observer Network

### What we claim

The CAR evidence measures **information permeability** of the observer network — not insider trading per se.

When material events occur at private companies with board observers, connected public companies show abnormal pre-event returns. The effect is:
- Concentrated in same-industry connections (information is industry-relevant)
- Strongest for the most material events (M&A, bankruptcy — not routine)
- Responsive to regulatory constraints (NVCA loosening amplified it, Clayton Act tightened it)

This is evidence that **information flows through observer networks and reaches prices** before public disclosure.

### What we don't claim

We deliberately don't identify HOW information reaches prices. Could be:
1. Direct trading by observer (Form 4 suggestive but underpowered)
2. Network diffusion through VC ecosystem (many small trades by loosely connected people)
3. Strategic decisions at Firm B influenced by observer-as-director
4. Market inference from VC ecosystem signals

### The right frame

Not "are observers insider trading?" but **"does the observer role create information channels that wouldn't exist under a traditional director-only board?"**

This connects to the accountability gap:
- Observers have same information as directors, no fiduciary duty
- Information constrained by fiduciary duty in director-only boards leaks through observer channel
- Regulatory response (Clayton Act 2025) validates this — DOJ/FTC extended Section 8 because the information channel is real

### Positioning vs prior literature

| Prior lit | Our contribution |
|---|---|
| Interlocking directorates (Kang 2008, Shi 2025) — public-to-public | **Private-to-public** — genuine information asymmetry |
| Post-event spillover (reputation, decision copying) | **Pre-event** — information leaks before disclosure |
| IVs for interlock formation (retirement age, meeting conflicts) | **Two regulatory shocks** affecting information constraints |
| Classic insider trading (Form 4 at own company) | **Cross-company** — observer at Firm A, effect at Firm B |
| Tipping literature (Ahern 2017 RFS) — ex-post SEC cases | **Ex-ante opportunity** — network structure predicts information flow |

**Why:** Frames the contribution clearly. Avoids overclaiming (not proving insider trading) while still showing the channel is real and economically significant.

**How to apply:** Use this framing in the introduction, results interpretation, and conclusion. Don't speculate about mechanism — present the evidence and let readers draw implications.
