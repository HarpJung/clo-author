| Method | Application | Key Assumption to Defend |
|--------|-------------|------------------------|
| Semi-structured interviews | 3 practitioners with board governance experience | Informants have direct knowledge of director/advisor/observer dynamics |

## Field Conventions

- Distinguish **fiduciary duties** (directors) from **advisory/contractual roles** (advisors, observers)
- Frame within **agency theory** (Jensen & Meckling) vs. **stewardship theory** (Davis, Schoorman, Donaldson)
- Note legal liability differences: directors have fiduciary duty; observers/advisors typically do not
- Address **information asymmetry** -- observers see board deliberations without voting power
- Specify **jurisdiction** (Delaware law governs most US public companies)
- Define roles precisely: "board advisor" and "board observer" lack standardized definitions in literature
- PE/VC context: observer rights are typically negotiated in investment agreements
- Connect to **monitoring vs. advising** framework (Adams & Ferreira 2007)

## Field-Specific Referee Concerns

- "Why not use archival/quantitative data?" -- qualitative approach needs strong justification (access to tacit knowledge, unexplored phenomenon)
- "How do you define board advisor vs. board observer?" -- no standardized definitions exist; must establish working definitions early
- "Selection bias in interview subjects" -- who are the 3 practitioners and why them?
- "Generalizability from 3 interviews" -- appropriate for exploratory/theory-building research; note plans for expansion
- "How is this different from advisory boards?" -- advisory boards are a separate structure from individual board advisors
- "Legal framework varies by jurisdiction" -- must specify (Delaware law, Model Business Corporation Act, etc.)
- "What's the corporate accountability angle?" -- connect to monitoring effectiveness, information flows, and stakeholder protection
- "Isn't this just about VC board seats?" -- must show phenomenon extends beyond PE/VC context (joint ventures, regulated industries, government-appointed observers)

## Notation Conventions

| Term | Definition | Anti-pattern |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| Board of Directors | Formally elected body with fiduciary duties and voting rights | Don't conflate with advisory board |
| Board Observer | Individual with contractual right to attend board meetings without voting | Don't assume observers have fiduciary duties |
| Board Advisor | Individual providing advisory services to the board without formal membership | Don't conflate with independent director or consultant |
| Board Governance System | The full configuration of directors + advisors + observers | Don't reduce to "board composition" (which usually means directors only) |
